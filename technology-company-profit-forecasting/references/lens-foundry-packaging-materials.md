# Lens: foundry, packaging, materials, and physical infrastructure

# Advanced packaging and OSAT playbook

Model package/test units, advanced package content, ASP, yield, utilization, material content, depreciation, customer qualification, customer concentration and capex. Separate advanced packaging from commodity packaging and test.

# Power and cooling playbook

Model orders, backlog quality, cancellation and deferral, project/product mix, delivery and acceptance, price/cost lag, manufacturing capacity, service, installation and working capital. AI data-center demand is a customer demand signal, not recognized revenue.

# Semiconductor materials and optics playbook

Materials: wafer starts × process steps × content per wafer × share. Optics: design wins × platform units × optical content. Add qualification, yield, utilization, price erosion, material cost and acquisition/perimeter bridges. Do not equate technical bandwidth demand with a named supplier win.


## Intel / IDM-transition lessons

- Separate product demand, process-node timing, manufacturing utilization, internal wafer economics and external foundry economics.
- Public capacity plans increase capital intensity before they prove customer revenue or returns.
- PC, data-center and manufacturing states can diverge.
- Exact future impairments, accelerated depreciation and restructuring charges generally belong in distribution tails unless the asset-level evidence was visible.
- Maintain reported and normalized segment/perimeter bridges through reorganizations.
