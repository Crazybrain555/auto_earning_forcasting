# Research completeness and company-quality protocol

## Purpose

A structurally valid workbook can still be a weak forecast if the research corpus is thin. This protocol prevents a run from passing merely because it contains enough source rows, report headings, formulas, and red-team items.

The quality target is to reproduce the full research chain expected from a strong technology-company analyst:

`history -> business/product map -> customers and demand -> supply/channel -> technology/IP -> competition/moat -> management/capital allocation -> accounting/cash -> scenarios -> valuation`

## Two independent axes

Every delivery must state two separate statuses:

- `process_integrity`: cutoff, evidence permissions, formulas, audit trail, strict validation and immutable snapshot;
- `research_sufficiency`: whether the accepted corpus and schedules are deep enough to support the numerical horizon.

A sealed and formula-correct run may still be `research-pack-insufficient`. Process integrity never upgrades research sufficiency.

## Required research artifacts

A full three- to five-year company forecast must maintain:

- `research_coverage_matrix.csv`;
- `company_quality_moat_register.csv`;
- `technology_commercialization_register.csv`;
- `product_customer_driver_schedule.csv`;
- `material_assumption_support.csv`;
- `research_completeness.json`, generated by the validator.

## Minimum source depth

For a public technology company, normally require:

1. Three historical annual filings or equivalent substantial extracts, with statement, segment, customer, product, accounting, cash-flow and risk anchors.
2. The latest earnings release and at least one substantial formal investor-dialogue source such as a transcript, Investor Day, conference Q&A, shareholder letter, or regulatory/company response.
3. At least two independent external evidence clusters across customers, suppliers, competitors, measured industry research, experts, standards, papers, patents, or high-quality news discovery.
4. Product/technology sources sufficient to map the material product roadmap and commercialization stages.
5. Management, governance and capital-allocation evidence sufficient to assess persistence and downside behaviour.

A one-paragraph summary of a filing is not a full filing source. Source records must state:

- `document_depth`: `full_document`, `substantial_excerpt`, or `summary_only`;
- `word_count`;
- `anchor_count`;
- `coverage_topics`;
- whether the original document or a reproducible sanitized extract is retained.

`summary_only` records may support navigation but do not satisfy the historical-filing or dialogue minimum.

## Research coverage matrix

The following topics are mandatory for a full company forecast:

- historical financial statements and accounting vintage;
- current quarter, guide and near-term bridge;
- business segments, products and revenue units;
- customers, channels, usage and demand;
- competitors and market structure;
- supply, capacity, delivery, inventory and cost;
- technology roadmap and product-stage evidence;
- papers, standards, patents and technical failure boundaries;
- management, governance and capital allocation;
- company quality and moat persistence;
- balance sheet, cash flow and economic capital;
- industry, policy and macro state;
- news and event timeline;
- valuation and reverse-implied expectations.

Each topic must record status, source IDs, independent clusters, depth, unresolved questions, financial-model link and readiness cap.

## Company quality and moat

Do not infer long-term growth or terminal margins solely from recent revenue. Review at least:

- management execution and disclosure quality;
- governance and incentives;
- capital allocation and M&A record;
- R&D productivity and product cadence;
- patents, proprietary technology and standards position;
- customer stickiness, switching costs and ecosystem;
- channel/distribution strength and channel risk;
- cost, scale, manufacturing or data advantage;
- competitive response and substitution risk;
- balance-sheet resilience and counterparty risk.

Every accepted moat claim must map to a forecast permission: share persistence, price, margin, reinvestment, downside probability, or normalized value.

## Technology, papers, patents and commercialization

For a three-year or longer technology forecast, a technology/IP review is mandatory even when the immediate risk is commercial. Route evidence through:

`paper/patent/standard -> benchmark -> prototype -> sample -> qualification -> production -> customer adoption -> material revenue`

The review must cover material products, competing architectures, relevant standards, patent/IP positioning, technical bottlenecks and commercialization stage. A conclusion of `not_material` requires a product-by-product reason; it cannot be a single generic sentence.

## Product, customer and driver schedules

Each material business line must have a schedule identifying:

- revenue-recognizing unit;
- product or service;
- customer/payer and end user;
- volume/usage/deployment driver;
- price/ARPU/ASP;
- mix/share/attach rate;
- cost and capacity constraint;
- product/program stage;
- evidence IDs and confidence;
- relationship to the consolidated model.

If quantity, price, mix or customer conversion cannot be measured, do not replace them with an unexplained generic CAGR. Mark the schedule `human_required`, widen the distribution, and cap the point-forecast horizon.

## Material assumption support

Record every conclusion-critical assumption with:

- materiality weight;
- sensitivity to revenue/profit/value;
- support status;
- independent evidence-cluster count;
- source IDs;
- allowed horizon;
- falsification trigger.

Support statuses:

- `hard_anchor`;
- `corroborated`;
- `single_source`;
- `analyst_only`;
- `human_required`.

For `screen-grade`, no more than 35% of materiality weight may be `analyst_only` or `human_required`, and no conclusion-critical Base driver may be supported by zero independent evidence clusters.

## Mechanism-specific readiness caps

### Unit-volume-price-mix-cost

Require a product/driver schedule with observable quantity, price, mix and cost anchors. If only aggregate revenue history exists, readiness is at most `hypothesis-grade` beyond FY+1.

### Channel or cycle-state hardware

Require current sell-through, channel inventory, order survival/cancellation, or another direct channel-state measurement. If the conclusion-critical channel state is unknown:

- FY+1 may retain a point with a wide range if officially guided;
- FY+2 and FY+3 must be scenario/distribution outputs rather than high-confidence points;
- readiness cannot exceed `hypothesis-grade`.

### Program-stage products

A product option cannot support Base until stage, customer evidence, units/content, price and expected recognition are documented.

## Validator philosophy

The validator checks research content, not prose volume. It must fail a package containing many tiny summaries, generic section headings, or a large number of unsupported analyst assumptions.

A run cannot claim parity with the gold-standard research method unless:

- material coverage topics are complete;
- source depth passes;
- products/customers/technology/company quality are linked to financial assumptions;
- unsupported materiality is below the threshold;
- mechanism-specific readiness caps are obeyed.
