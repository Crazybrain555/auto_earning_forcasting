# Lens: subscription and content platform

Use this lens after selecting `module-subscriber-content-economics.md`. It is not a separate Skill.

## Data checklist

- paid memberships, average paid memberships, net additions and any disclosed churn/engagement;
- ARPU by region/plan, price changes, plan mix, FX and bundles;
- mature versus scaling-region contribution economics;
- content amortization, cash additions, liabilities and off-balance obligations;
- marketing/customer-acquisition costs and partner payments;
- debt, interest, content production capacity and release schedule;
- advertising, paid-sharing, games, live, or other monetization stage evidence.

## Netflix retrospective lessons

1. Domestic/UCAN and international cohorts must be separated; newer markets can suppress operating profit while increasing long-term scale.
2. Average paid memberships and ARPU explain revenue better than ending memberships alone.
3. International contribution inflection can make operating leverage nonlinear.
4. Cash content payments can exceed amortization by billions for years; operating profit and FCF may diverge materially.
5. Pandemic demand and production-payment shifts were exogenous. Historical Base forecasts must not be rewritten to assume them.
6. Content release timing affects acquisition, churn, amortization, cash payments and operating margin at different times.

## Readiness

Revenue and operating income may be `research-grade` when regional membership, ARPU, contribution and content schedules are sufficiently disclosed. Full FCF/valuation remains `screen-grade` if churn cohorts, title economics, obligations timing, or debt/content allocation are material and undisclosed.
