# Validated coverage and readiness

## Retrospectively validated mechanisms

The bundled cases provide varying retrospective support for:

- unit-volume-price-cost and cycle-state in memory/storage;
- capacity/utilization/yield in foundry, packaging, and manufacturing;
- orders/backlog/recognition and installed-base service in equipment;
- process-content intensity in inspection/metrology;
- BOM pass-through and working capital in servers;
- project backlog in power/cooling;
- program-stage conversion and perimeter/GAAP bridges in optical/custom silicon;
- platform usage/adoption in cloud infrastructure;
- subscriber/ARPU/region/content economics in subscription-content platforms;
- channel inventory, supply commitments, perimeter and regime tails in compute platforms.

This is retrospective simulation, not live decision-grade performance.

## Cloud platform validation

AWS multi-cutoff retrospective holdouts support segment revenue and segment operating income. Standalone cloud-segment FCF, ROIC and full valuation remain `screen-grade` and `human-required` without working-capital, lease, tax, shared-asset/cost, cash and capital-structure bridges.

## Subscription/content validation

Netflix multi-cutoff retrospective tests support company revenue and operating income using paid membership, ARPU, regional maturity, contribution economics and content amortization/cash bridges. Exact churn/gross-add cohorts, title-level content ROI, obligation timing and full cash-content valuation remain `screen-grade` and `human-required` when undisclosed.

## Compute-platform validation

NVIDIA multi-cutoff tests support market-platform segmentation, channel inventory, acquisition perimeter, supply/inventory provisions, GAAP bridges and regime-tail outputs. Specific future platform-break magnitude remains distributional; unannounced acquisitions and conclusion-critical supply/customer allocations remain `human-required`.

## Unvalidated domains

SaaS, marketplaces, advertising platforms, biotech, finance and unrelated industries require dedicated mechanism validation. Reuse the universal Source Pack, accounting, scenario, valuation, backtest and governance workflow, but do not claim the operating equation is validated.


## Forward-evidence layer — v7.4
Micron 2018/2020, Marvell 2020 and NVIDIA 2020 retrospective ablations indicate that measured price/inventory evidence can improve cycle timing; investor dialogue modestly improves execution estimates; technical papers mainly improve feasibility bounds and regime tails. Expert/sell-side weights remain human-required without complete historical methods and source sets.


## Expanded statistical registry — v7.5

The unified retrospective registry now includes AMD and Intel multi-cutoff cases plus the previously validated equipment, networking, cloud, subscription, compute, memory and cross-value-chain panels.

AMD extends product-platform ramps, competitive share, public/unannounced perimeter changes and acquisition-accounting bridges. Intel extends process-roadmap risk, PC/data-center cycle divergence, IDM/foundry capital intensity, long-duration operating deterioration and impairment tails.

The registry must be evaluated with company-cluster bootstrap, robust statistics and influence tests. The cross-company LOCO calibration remains diagnostic only because it slightly degrades several company panels. `automatic_global_prior_allowed=false`.
