# Lens: compute platforms and diversified technology

Use inside the unified Skill; this is not a separate Skill.

## Segment and economic-unit map

Separate Data Center compute, networking/interconnect, Gaming, Professional Visualization, Automotive, OEM/IP, systems, software/services, and acquisitions. Confirm whether revenue is chip, board, system, networking equipment, license, or service.

## Channel and demand bridge

```text
Sell-in revenue
= End demand / sell-through
+ Change in distributor / OEM / retailer inventory
+ New-product launch fill
- Returns / pricing programs / channel corrections
```

Track:

- sell-in versus sell-through;
- distributor and OEM inventory;
- cryptocurrency or other non-core demand contamination;
- product-generation launch fill and old-generation runoff;
- customer concentration and direct versus channel customers.

Do not infer end demand from sell-in alone.

## Platform adoption and supply

```text
Platform revenue
= Eligible deployments
× Content per deployment
× Supplier share
× ASP
× Supply availability
× Stage / adoption probability
```

Supply schedules must consider foundry wafers, advanced packaging, HBM/memory, substrates, networking, systems, long lead-time purchase obligations and inventory commitments.

## Perimeter and acquisition bridge

If a transaction is public by `as_of`, model close probability, partial consolidation, target standalone growth, financing, dilution, inventory step-up, amortization, integration and restructuring. If it is not public, preserve the organic Base and use a distribution-only perimeter tail; never infer the later transaction by name.

## GAAP bridge

```text
GAAP operating income
= Segment/platform operating economics
- acquired-intangible amortization
- inventory step-up / excess inventory / purchase-obligation provisions
- acquisition termination / integration / restructuring
- SBC and corporate platform investment
± accounting-estimate effects
```

Publish both reported and normalized profit when material one-offs or accounting-estimate changes exist.

## Cycle and regime states

- channel correction / destocking;
- normal platform ramp;
- supply constrained;
- customer-capex pause;
- platform/ecosystem regime break;
- mature platform normalization.

For regime breaks, use discrete probability branches and convex tails. Base
probability may increase only when proposition-appropriate evidence from
independent measurement roots resolves the important rival explanations—for
example material revenue, customer deployment/capex, supply commitments or
multi-customer production adoption. Do not convert a fixed source count or an
E-label into forecast permission.

## NVIDIA retrospective lessons

1. Gaming sell-in was distorted by cryptocurrency demand and channel inventory; a platform model without a channel bridge missed the FY2020 decline and FY2023 correction.
2. Mellanox expanded Data Center and networking economics, but only after the acquisition was publicly announced and probability-weighted.
3. Arm was public but uncertain; the later termination charge belonged in the GAAP/perimeter branch, not a deterministic operating forecast.
4. Data Center compute and networking must be separated from Gaming and OEM/other.
5. Inventory provisions, excess purchase obligations and reserve releases can move gross margin materially.
6. Generative-AI acceleration was a regime tail at earlier cutoffs, not a point forecast that could be backfilled from later results.

## Human-required items

Use `human-required` when channel inventory, customer commitments, supply obligations, foundry/packaging/HBM allocation, acquisition probability, or platform revenue boundaries are conclusion-critical and not verifiable.


## AMD lessons

- Model Client/Gaming/Data Center/Embedded separately.
- A product roadmap or benchmark does not establish server share; use customer deployment, channel sell-through and supply.
- Public acquisitions require a perimeter and GAAP purchase-accounting bridge.
- Transactions not public at the cutoff remain anonymous distribution tails.
- Preserve the financial vintage available at the cutoff rather than silently replacing it with later recast comparatives.
