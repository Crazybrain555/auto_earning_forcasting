# Methodological Foundations

This registry records the narrow proposition each authoritative source supports
and the boundary that prevents overclaiming. It is not an appeal-to-authority
substitute for company evidence.  It is also not a reading quota or universal
syllabus: use an entry only when it improves the causal, data or accounting
judgment that carries future revenue, operating profit or attributable net
income for the active company.

## Model governance, preventive design and effective challenge

### Federal Reserve, OCC and FDIC revised model-risk guidance (2026)

Sources:

- [SR 26-2 — Revised Guidance on Model Risk Management](https://www.federalreserve.gov/supervisionreg/srletters/SR2602.htm)
- [Supervisory Guidance on Model Risk Management](https://www.federalreserve.gov/frrs/guidance/supervisory-guidance-on-model-risk-management.htm)

Permitted use: support beginning with an explicit model purpose and intended
decision; making development and testing proportionate to material use; testing
conceptual soundness, implementation, monitoring and realized outcomes as
distinct questions; recording limitations and common data/assumption
dependencies; and using technically competent, substantively independent
`effective challenge` throughout the model lifecycle.  It also supports the
important distinction that an internally accurate model can remain dangerous
when used outside its purpose.

Misuse boundary: this is supervisory guidance for banking organizations, not a
public-equity research standard or evidence that this forecasting skill is a
regulated model-risk program.  It prescribes no investment thesis, company
driver, test count, pass score, sample size or universal materiality threshold.
Its validation concepts strengthen governance; they do not turn validation
into a substitute for economically sound model construction.

### PCAOB AS 2201

Source: [AS 2201 — An Audit of Internal Control Over Financial Reporting](https://pcaobus.org/oversight/standards/auditing-standards/details/as-2201--an-audit-of-internal-control-over-financial-reporting-that-is-integrated-with-an-audit-of-financial-statements-%28effective-on-12-15-2026%29)

Permitted use: support separating design effectiveness from operating
effectiveness and distinguishing preventive from detective controls.  By
analogy, a forecasting method should prevent duplicate sources of truth,
invalid state transitions and unsupported downstream inputs through its SOP,
types and generated views before relying on validators to detect residual
failures.  Testing remains risk-based and tied to a specific assertion.

Misuse boundary: the forecasting skill is not an audit or an internal-control
system, and a passing validator provides no audit assurance.  AS 2201 does not
say that preventive controls eliminate substantive testing, that every risk
needs a separate test, or that a larger test suite is a better model.

### SEC Staff Accounting Bulletin No. 99

Source: [SAB No. 99 —
Materiality](https://www.sec.gov/interps/account/sab99.htm)

Permitted use: support a context-sensitive materiality decision.  A numerical
threshold can be an initial screen, but size is only the beginning of the
analysis; nature, surrounding circumstances and whether an item changes a
trend, segment, contractual boundary or investor inference also matter.  By
analogy, the research SOP should spend effort on facts, identities and tests
capable of changing the revenue, operating-profit or attributable-income
conclusion, even when the affected amount looks small in isolation.

Misuse boundary: SAB 99 addresses financial-statement misstatements under US
securities law and is staff guidance, not a forecasting-test design standard.
It supplies neither a fixed tolerance nor permission to call every qualitative
issue material.  It rejects exclusive reliance on a number; it does not reject
quantification, accounting identities or precision-aware tolerances.  Do not
turn its qualitative examples into another mandatory checklist or score.

## Model construction and analyst practice

### FAST Standard

Sources:

- [FAST Standard overview](https://www.fast-standard.org/the-fast-standard/)
- [FAST Standard, edition 02c](https://www.fast-standard.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/FAST-Standard-02c-July-2019.pdf)

Permitted use: support readable, structured, consistent and transparent
spreadsheet construction, including clear calculation flow and reviewability.

Misuse boundary: FAST is a model-engineering convention. It does not establish
the truth of an operating assumption, determine intrinsic value or validate a
forecasting method by itself.

### ICAEW 20 Principles for Good Spreadsheet Practice

Source: [ICAEW 20 Principles, 2024 edition](https://www.icaew.com/technical/technology/excel-community/20-principles-for-good-spreadsheet-practice-2024-edition)

Permitted use: support proportionate design, separation of inputs and
calculations, documentation, testing, version control and independent review.

Misuse boundary: the principles govern spreadsheet risk. They do not prescribe
a company-specific causal model or replace accounting and evidence work.

### Brown, Call, Clement and Sharp (2015)

Source: [Inside the Black Box of Sell-Side Financial Analysts](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/1475-679X.12067)

Permitted use: motivate explicit treatment of analyst information gathering,
industry knowledge, management access and judgment as parts of forecast
practice that are not visible in a published estimate.

Misuse boundary: interview evidence about analyst practice is not proof that a
particular source is unbiased or that any sell-side model is accurate. This
method still requires source lineage, causal permission and validation.

## Evidence authority, management and analyst judgment

### PCAOB AS 1105, AS 2501 and AS 2805

Sources:

- [AS 1105 — Audit Evidence](https://pcaobus.org/oversight/standards/auditing-standards/details/AS1105)
- [AS 2501 — Auditing Accounting Estimates, Including Fair Value
  Measurements](https://pcaobus.org/oversight/standards/auditing-standards/details/AS2501)
- [AS 2805 — Management Representations](https://pcaobus.org/oversight/standards/auditing-standards/details/AS2805)

Permitted use: support proposition-specific tests of relevance and reliability,
preservation and investigation of contradictory evidence, and the distinction
between a management representation and evidence obtained through other
procedures.  For material estimates, separately evaluate the data, assumptions
and method, form an independent expectation or evidence-supported range where
useful, and explain the difference from the company's estimate. Evidence
quality, not repetition of the same source type, drives the need for additional
work.

Misuse boundary: a public-equity forecast is not an audit.  These standards do
not create a universal second-source quota, make management unreliable, or let
an external source override management on management's own stated intent.  The
precise proposition, authority scope, definition and future-execution bridge
still determine permission.

### PCAOB AS 1210 on specialists

Source: [AS 1210 — Using the Work of an Auditor-Engaged
Specialist](https://pcaobus.org/oversight/standards/auditing-standards/details/AS1210)

Permitted use: provide questions for using an expert or specialist: is the
person competent for this exact field and task, what relationships or
incentives affect objectivity, what assertion was addressed, and which data,
significant assumptions and methods produced the conclusion?  The model may
use specialist work to define a hypothesis, technical boundary, failure mode
or evidence-supported parameter only after those elements are visible and fit
the model proposition.

Misuse boundary: AS 1210 governs auditor-engaged specialists; an expert call,
industry consultant, sell-side analyst or academic is not thereby an audit
specialist.  Credentials, reputation and apparent independence do not make an
opinion a measurement of customer demand, future execution or profit.  Do not
convert competence, objectivity, method and data into points, require a fixed
number of experts, or let expert consensus replace proposition-specific facts
and a causal bridge.

### SEC MD&A guidance and the 2025 IFRS Management Commentary Practice Statement

Sources:

- [SEC Commission Guidance Regarding Management's Discussion and Analysis of
  Financial Condition and Results of
  Operations](https://www.sec.gov/rules-regulations/2003/12/commission-guidance-regarding-managements-discussion-analysis-financial-condition-results-operations)
- [IFRS Practice Statement 1 — Management Commentary (revised June
  2025)](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/management-commentary-practice-statement-1/)
- [IASB project summary for the revised Practice
  Statement](https://www.ifrs.org/content/dam/ifrs/project/management-commentary/practice-statement/projectsummary-mc-ps1.pdf)

Permitted use: treat issuer commentary as direct evidence of management's
stated perspective, strategy, monitored key matters, claimed internal state and
identified known trends or uncertainties.  The IFRS objectives-based framework
also provides a useful causal reading order—external environment, business
model, strategy, resources and relationships, risks, then financial performance
and position—and asks that related statements, metrics and public
communications be coherent.  This supports recovering company-specific
operating metrics and testing whether management's explanation connects to the
reported statements and to future cash-generation mechanisms.

Misuse boundary: management commentary is a fact-based narrative from
management's perspective, not independent proof that the plan will execute,
that customers will act as claimed or that an external market state exists.
The revised IFRS Practice Statement is not an IFRS Accounting Standard and does
not require every company to publish a forecast or target.  Official status is
proposition-scoped: it anchors what the issuer reported or represented; crossing
from that representation to future revenue, operating profit or attributable
income requires an observable causal or external test.  Do not force the six
content areas, examples or metrics into a universal company checklist.

### SEC guidance on analyst recommendations

Source: [Analyzing Analyst Recommendations](https://www.sec.gov/about/reports-publications/investorpubsanalystshtm)

Permitted use: require disclosure and review of research conflicts, distinguish
recommendations from underlying facts and methods, and recover the original
evidence before an opinion affects a Base assumption.

Misuse boundary: the existence of a potential conflict does not prove that a
recommendation or estimate is wrong.  Do not apply a mechanical optimism
haircut, reject all sell-side work, or count agreeing reports as independent
measurements.

### Jegadeesh and Kim (2010), Hirshleifer et al. (2018), and Ma et al. (2020)

Sources:

- [Do Analysts Herd?](https://www.nber.org/papers/w12866)
- [Decision Fatigue and Heuristic Analyst Forecasts](https://www.nber.org/papers/w24293)
- [A Quantitative Analysis of Distortions in Managerial Forecasts](https://www.nber.org/papers/w26830)

Permitted use: motivate explicit common-origin and incentive checks, retention
of company-specific forecast-error histories, and challenger tests against
consensus or management forecasts instead of treating either as truth.

Misuse boundary: population evidence does not establish the bias, direction or
accuracy of a particular analyst, manager, issuer, horizon or regime.  It
supports calibration and counter-hypotheses, not a generic adjustment
coefficient.

### de Silva and Thesmar (2021) and van Binsbergen, Han and Lopez-Lira (2021)

Sources:

- [Noise in Expectations: Evidence from Analyst
  Forecasts](https://www.nber.org/papers/w28963)
- [Man vs. Machine Learning: The Term Structure of Earnings Expectations and
  Conditional Biases](https://www.nber.org/papers/w27843)

Permitted use: motivate horizon-specific treatment of analyst estimates and a
simple, point-in-time statistical challenger. Analysts may add information at
short horizons while noise and conditional bias can grow with horizon; compare
like-for-like horizons instead of granting or denying blanket authority.

Misuse boundary: neither paper proves that the machine or analyst is superior
for this issuer, regime or data set. The challenger cannot replace the causal
profit model, and a measured population bias is not a universal haircut.

### Bloom, Codreanu and Fletcher (2025, revised 2026)

Source: [Rationalizing Firm Forecasts](https://www.nber.org/papers/w33384)

Permitted use: treat over-precision, predictable error and weak persistence of
one-off process improvements as live rival explanations; preserve forecast
ranges, vintages and repeated holdout evidence rather than assuming that a
training intervention permanently improved judgment.

Misuse boundary: the surveyed firms and interventions are not this forecasting
system. The study does not set an interval width, calibration coefficient or
promotion sample size, and it cannot establish that a particular management
forecast is biased.

### PCAOB AS 2305 on analytical procedures

Source: [AS 2305 — Substantive Analytical
Procedures](https://pcaobus.org/oversight/standards/auditing-standards/details/AS2305)

Permitted use: discipline a cross-check around the exact assertion, a plausible
and sufficiently predictable relationship, reliable data and an expectation
precise enough to expose an informative residual.  Relevant relationships can
link financial and non-financial observations—for example, price, volume and
mix to revenue, or headcount and compensation to personnel cost—and an
unexpected residual must be investigated rather than averaged away.  Greater
detail can improve diagnostic power when the definitions and boundary remain
comparable.

Misuse boundary: this is an auditing standard, not evidence that an analytical
relationship proves a public-equity forecast or detects fraud.  Apparent
correlation can be spurious, unstable regimes make relationships less
predictable, and offsetting drivers can conceal errors.  Use only relationships
with an economic or accounting reason; do not require a fixed equation count,
duplicate an identity in several tests, or infer causality from a small
residual.  Tests remain orthogonal questions, not a weighted quality score.

## Forecast evaluation, persistence and earnings quality

### Fama and French (2000)

Source: [Forecasting Profitability and Earnings](https://doi.org/10.1086/209638)

Permitted use: establish mean reversion in profitability as an empirical
outside-view hypothesis and motivate nonlinear, state-conditional reference
classes.

Misuse boundary: the population estimate is not a company fade rate.  Do not
copy the paper's average speed, use a generic industry mean, or override
contracts, capacity response, lifecycle and accounting facts.

### Sloan (1996)

Source: [Do Stock Prices Fully Reflect Information in Accruals and Cash Flows
About Future Earnings?](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2598)

Permitted use: support separating the cash and accrual components of earnings
when assessing persistence.

Misuse boundary: current accrual magnitude is neither an earnings-quality score
nor a universal haircut.  Growth, working capital, conservative accounting,
perimeter and estimate reliability determine the company interpretation.

### Richardson, Sloan, Soliman and Tuna (2005)

Source: [Accrual Reliability, Earnings Persistence and Stock
Prices](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jacceco.2005.04.005)

Permitted use: motivate a balance-sheet decomposition of accruals and explicit
attention to estimate reliability and reversal.

Misuse boundary: the empirical categories do not set company-specific
persistence coefficients and do not prove manipulation.

### Dechow, Ge and Schrand (2010)

Source: [Understanding earnings quality: a review of the proxies, their
determinants and their consequences](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jacceco.2010.09.001)

Permitted use: require the analyst to define the decision context before using a
quality proxy and to separate fundamental performance from measurement effects.

Misuse boundary: no single proxy establishes "high-quality" earnings.  A
composite score would conceal construct and accounting-policy differences.

### Penman and Zhang (2002)

Source: [Accounting Conservatism, the Quality of Earnings, and Stock
Returns](https://doi.org/10.2139/SSRN.201048)

Permitted use: test whether changing investment under conservative accounting
temporarily depresses or releases reported earnings and RNOA.

Misuse boundary: expensed R&D, advertising or customer acquisition is not
automatically an asset.  Economic life and commercial value must be evidenced
and sensitivity-tested.

### Lev and Sougiannis (1996)

Source: [The capitalization, amortization, and value-relevance of R&D](https://doi.org/10.1016/0165-4101(95)00410-6)

Permitted use: motivate vintage-cohort sensitivity analysis when material R&D
investment is expensed and may distort reported reinvestment and profitability
comparability.

Misuse boundary: value relevance is not proof of future commercial value or
forecast accuracy. Do not capitalize all R&D, copy a population life, or
overwrite the issuer's reported accounting.

### Fairfield and Yohn (2001)

Source: [Using Asset Turnover and Profit Margin to Forecast Changes in
Profitability](https://doi.org/10.1023/A:1012430513430)

Permitted use: motivate analysis of changes in margin and turnover, not only
their levels, when forecasting changes in profitability.

Misuse boundary: DuPont components remain diagnostics.  The active company's
price, mix, utilization, working-capital and investment causes still need a
causal bridge.

### Anderson, Banker and Janakiraman (2003)

Source: [Are Selling, General, and Administrative Costs
"Sticky"?](https://doi.org/10.1111/1475-679X.00095)

Permitted use: reject automatic symmetric cost elasticity and model resource
commitments, adjustment lags and exit costs separately in up and down states.

Misuse boundary: published population coefficients are not company parameters.
Estimate or scenario-test company activity drivers, commitments and management
responses.

### Gneiting and Raftery (2007)

Source: [Strictly Proper Scoring Rules, Prediction, and
Estimation](https://doi.org/10.1198/016214506000001437)

Permitted use: score probabilistic forecasts with rules that reward calibrated,
honest distributions and penalize both misses and unnecessarily wide intervals.

Misuse boundary: one score does not explain an error.  Preserve point errors,
coverage, interval width, sign, data revisions and causal attribution separately.

### Gneiting (2011)

Source: [Making and Evaluating Point Forecasts](https://doi.org/10.1198/jasa.2011.r10138)

Permitted use: require a point forecast to state the statistical functional it
targets—such as a mean, median or quantile—and match evaluation loss to that
functional.  In this system the canonical point remains the executable
reference joint path; any probability-weighted expectation or quantile is a
separately labeled distribution summary.

Misuse boundary: the paper does not select the company's reference scenario,
loss preferences or probabilities.  A statistically optimal marginal point
can be an impossible combination of revenue, cost, tax and NCI states, so it
does not replace execution of coherent joint financial paths.

### Gneiting, Balabdaoui and Raftery (2007)

Source: [Probabilistic forecasts, calibration and sharpness](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-9868.2007.00587.x)

Permitted use: require forecast distributions and scenario probabilities to be
audited for calibration and sharpness and to represent realizable joint paths.

Misuse boundary: the paper does not supply company scenario probabilities,
cross-line correlations or operating assumptions. Marginally calibrated
revenue and profit intervals can still be an incoherent joint financial path.

### Wickramasuriya, Athanasopoulos and Hyndman (2019)

Source: [Optimal Forecast Reconciliation for Hierarchical and Grouped Time Series](https://doi.org/10.1080/01621459.2018.1448825)

Permitted use: motivate preserving aggregation identities when forecasts are
reported at multiple levels. Segment, period and profit-chain scenario outputs
must reconcile.

Misuse boundary: statistical reconciliation cannot repair an incorrect causal
model, accounting perimeter or scenario shock, and the paper supplies no
company covariance matrix.

### Tashman (2000) and Giacomini and White (2006)

Sources:

- [Out-of-sample tests of forecasting accuracy](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0169-2070(00)00065-0)
- [Tests of Conditional Predictive Ability](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1468-0262.2006.00718.x)

Permitted use: preserve forecast origins, horizons, temporal dependence and
economic regimes, and assess whether relative performance depends on the state.

Misuse boundary: two cases do not establish statistical significance. Multiple
overlapping cutoffs for one issuer remain one economic cluster; these sources
do not prescribe a universal minimum sample size.

## Accounting-based valuation and growth

### Nissim and Penman (2001)

Source: [Ratio Analysis and Equity Valuation](https://business.columbia.edu/sites/default/files-efs/imce-uploads/dnissim/publications/Ratio_analysis_and_equity_valuation_From_research_to_practice.pdf)

Permitted use: support reformulating financial statements into operating and
financing activities and connecting profitability, turnover, leverage and
growth analysis to forecasting and valuation.

Misuse boundary: historical ratios are diagnostic decompositions, not stable
forecast constants. Accounting classifications and business economics must be
reconciled company by company.

### Ohlson (1995)

Source: [Earnings, Book Values, and Dividends in Equity Valuation](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1911-3846.1995.tb00461.x)

Permitted use: support residual-income valuation as a coherent bridge from book
value and abnormal earnings to equity value under its assumptions.

Misuse boundary: residual-income outputs are not automatically reliable when
book value, clean-surplus relations, persistence or accounting forecasts are
mis-specified. It is a valuation method and cross-check, not an empirical moat
test.

### Penman and Sougiannis (1998)

Source: [A Comparison of Dividend, Cash Flow, and Earnings Approaches to Equity Valuation](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1911-3846.1998.tb00564.x)

Permitted use: motivate reconciling dividend, cash-flow and accrual-accounting
valuation implementations and diagnosing finite-horizon terminal effects.

Misuse boundary: the study does not make one method universally superior, nor
does it authorize averaging methods with inconsistent assumptions.

### Damodaran on fundamental growth

Source: [Fundamental growth](https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar/New_Home_Page/valquestions/growth.htm)

Permitted use: support the relation between expected growth, reinvestment and
return on invested capital or equity, with definitions matched to the claim.

Misuse boundary: reinvestment rate × return is an identity only under consistent
measures. Acquisitions, working capital, expensed investment, leverage and
accounting changes require explicit bridges.

### Mauboussin and Callahan on ROIC and competitive-advantage duration

Sources:

- [Return on Invested Capital: How to Calculate ROIC and Handle Common
  Issues](https://www.morganstanley.com/im/publication/insights/articles/article_returnoninvestedcapital.pdf)
- [Competitive Advantage Period: The Neglected Value
  Driver](https://www.morganstanley.com/im/publication/insights/articles/article_theneglectedvaluedriver_ltr.pdf)

Permitted use: connect NOPAT, invested capital, incremental investment,
intangible-investment adjustments, competitive strategy and the duration of a
positive ROIC spread. Use market-implied advantage duration as a challenger to
the operating model and make fade a causal, debatable assumption.

Misuse boundary: historical or population ROIC persistence is not a company
moat or fade coefficient. ROIC can be distorted by accounting, acquisition,
capital-light and denominator effects; the forecast still needs segment
economics, incremental returns, competitor response and an explicit
reinvestment bridge.

### McKinsey on growth and ROIC

Source: [Balancing ROIC and growth to build
value](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/strategy-and-corporate-finance/our-insights/balancing-roic-and-growth-to-build-value)

Permitted use: challenge the idea that revenue growth is valuable by itself and
route the investment question through the interaction of growth, return on
incremental capital and the company's current economic state.

Misuse boundary: cross-company historical group results are not a forecast
rule, a hurdle-rate estimate or proof that current high ROIC persists. Company
opportunity sets, intangible investment, cycle and competitive response remain
case-specific.

### Berkshire Hathaway on business quality and moats

Source: [Berkshire Hathaway 2007 shareholder
letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2007ltr.pdf)

Permitted use: frame business quality as favorable long-term economics
protected against competitive attack, and distinguish durable structural
advantages from manager-dependent or temporarily high-return businesses.

Misuse boundary: a moat metaphor is not a checklist or score. Brand, scale,
switching cost or low cost matters only when an observable mechanism preserves
price, share, unit economics, reinvestment returns or cash conversion despite
competitor response; technology change can shorten rather than lengthen the
advantage period.

## Financial reporting anchors

### Applicable framework, version and issuer policy

Sources:

- [FASB Accounting Standards Codification access](https://fasb.org/page/PageContent?isStaticPage=true&pageId=%2Fstaticpages%2Fcodification-access.html)
- [FASB Accounting Standards Update effective dates](https://fasb.org/standards/accounting-standard-updated-effective-date)
- [IFRS issued standards](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/)
- [PRC Accounting Standard for Business Enterprises No. 14 — Revenue](https://kjs.mof.gov.cn/zt/kjzzss/kuaijizhunzeshishi/201709/t20170907_2694006.htm)
- [PRC Accounting Standard for Business Enterprises No. 6 — Intangible Assets](https://kjs.mof.gov.cn/zt/kjzzss/kuaijizhunzeshishi/200806/t20080618_46242.htm)

Permitted use: identify the issuer's applicable framework, jurisdiction,
effective version, transition or early adoption, and material policy choices
for recognition, measurement and presentation.

Misuse boundary: `GAAP` is not one cross-jurisdiction definition. A standard
does not prove economic value or earnings persistence, and cross-framework
comparison still requires a quantified, sourced comparability bridge.

### IFRS Conceptual Framework for Financial Reporting

Source: [IFRS Conceptual Framework](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/conceptual-framework/)

Permitted use: anchor relevance, faithful representation, comparability,
verifiability and timeliness, and keep reporting-entity and measurement
boundaries explicit.

Misuse boundary: these characteristics do not make a document's numeric series
fit the model.  Construct, population, period, unit and vintage still require an
observation-level record.

### IMF Data Quality Assessment Framework

Source: [IMF Data Quality Reference Site](https://dsbb.imf.org/dqrs/DQAF)

Permitted use: motivate separate review of methodological soundness, accuracy
and reliability, serviceability, accessibility and revision practice for
external statistical series.

Misuse boundary: DQAF was not designed to score a company forecast.  This
method borrows its questions, not a numeric rating or presumption that official
data fit a company driver.

### SEC EDGAR structured-data documentation

Source: [SEC EDGAR APIs](https://www.sec.gov/search-filings/edgar-application-programming-interfaces)

Permitted use: define reproducible access to submissions and XBRL company facts
while retaining accession, filing time, period, dimensions and units.

Misuse boundary: an API fact may use the wrong dimension, fiscal period, sign,
scale or latest-restated history.  Material facts are checked against the
rendered filing and statement identities.

### SEC guidance on KPIs and operating metrics

Source: [Commission Guidance on Key Performance Indicators and Metrics in
MD&A](https://www.sec.gov/rules/interp/2020/33-10751.pdf)

Permitted use: require a material operating KPI to preserve its definition,
calculation method, management use, period, scope and changes in presentation
before it is joined across periods or mapped into a driver node.  A definition
change creates a new version and an explicit comparability bridge.

Misuse boundary: management use and disclosure do not make a KPI a GAAP fact,
causal driver or comparable peer measure.  A discontinued KPI is unknown after
its last valid period unless another observation supports a bridge; it is not
zero or evidence of deterioration by itself.

### SEC non-GAAP financial-measure interpretations

Source: [Non-GAAP Financial Measures — Compliance and Disclosure
Interpretations](https://www.sec.gov/rules-regulations/staff-guidance/corporation-finance-interpretations/non-gaap-financial-measures)

Permitted use: preserve the most comparable GAAP measure, label and definition,
reconcile each adjustment and its tax effect, track definition changes and
test whether a recurring operating cost or individually tailored recognition
basis is being excluded.  Management guidance in a non-GAAP construct never
replaces the system's GAAP operating-profit and attributable-income targets.

Misuse boundary: the interpretations govern issuer disclosure, not an
automatic forecast adjustment.  A non-GAAP measure can contain useful operating
information, while a reconciliation exception for a forward-looking measure
signals a GAAP limitation rather than permission for the analyst to invent the
missing amounts.

### IFRS 18 — Presentation and Disclosure in Financial Statements

Source: [IFRS
18](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs-18-presentation-and-disclosure-in-financial-statements/)

Permitted use: for IFRS reporters applying the standard, anchor the defined
operating-profit and profit-before-financing-and-income-taxes subtotals,
operating/investing/financing categories and disclosures about
management-defined performance measures.  Preserve the issuer's transition
date and recast comparatives, and reconcile management-defined measures to the
applicable IFRS subtotal before judging operating leverage or earnings power.
IFRS 18 is effective for annual periods beginning on or after 1 January 2027,
with earlier application permitted, so the basis must be versioned by issuer
and period.

Misuse boundary: IFRS 18 does not make pre-adoption issuer-defined operating
profit comparable, does not apply automatically to US GAAP or PRC ASBE
reporters, and does not prove that a management-defined adjustment is economic
or persistent.  Presentation categories are not causal drivers.  Do not splice
pre- and post-transition series without a sourced bridge or overwrite the
system's complete revenue-to-attributable-income chain with an adjusted
subtotal.

### IAS 34 — Interim Financial Reporting

Source: [IAS
34](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ias-34-interim-financial-reporting/)

Permitted use: preserve the difference between an interim period, a fiscal
year-to-date measurement and a full-year amount.  Apply the relevant accounting
policy consistently or record the disclosed policy change, retain comparative
periods, and flag that interim measurements generally use more estimation than
annual statements.  A significant later change in an estimate is a revision to
explain, not evidence that the earlier point-in-time observation never existed.

Misuse boundary: IAS 34 does not require every IFRS issuer to publish interim
reports and does not make an interim amount a mechanically seasonal run-rate.
Do not add four year-to-date quarters, compare a single quarter with a
year-to-date period, or score an earlier forecast against a later annual
restatement without preserving both vintages.  Its materiality and disclosure
requirements do not supply a universal quarterly tolerance or cadence.

### IFRS 15 — Revenue from Contracts with Customers

Source: [IFRS 15](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs-15-revenue-from-contracts-with-customers/)

Permitted use: anchor analysis of customer contracts, performance obligations,
transaction price and the timing of revenue recognition.

Misuse boundary: orders, backlog, billings, RPO, shipment and cash are not
interchangeable with revenue. Company disclosures and contract facts determine
the applicable bridge.

### IAS 7 — Statement of Cash Flows

Source: [IAS 7](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ias-7-statement-of-cash-flows.html)

Permitted use: anchor operating, investing and financing cash-flow
classification and the reconciliation of cash changes.

Misuse boundary: reported operating cash flow is not automatically owner
earnings; maintenance investment, factoring, supplier finance, acquisitions and
classification choices still require analysis.

### IAS 2 — Inventories

Source: [IAS 2](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ias-2-inventories/)

Permitted use: anchor inventory cost, net-realizable-value and write-down
analysis.

Misuse boundary: accounting inventory does not reveal channel inventory,
sell-through, obsolescence cause or future pricing without additional evidence.

### IAS 16 — Property, Plant and Equipment

Source: [IAS 16](https://www.ifrs.org/content/dam/ifrs/publications/pdf-standards/english/2022/issued/part-a/ias-16-property-plant-and-equipment.pdf)

Permitted use: anchor recognition, depreciation, useful lives, impairment and
disposal analysis for PPE.

Misuse boundary: accounting depreciation is not necessarily economic
maintenance investment, and announced capex is not productive capacity.

### IAS 38 — Intangible Assets

Source: [IAS 38](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ias-38-intangible-assets/)

Permitted use: anchor recognition and measurement distinctions for research,
development and intangible assets.

Misuse boundary: expensed R&D has economic content even when no asset is
recognized; capitalization does not prove commercial value.

### IFRS 8 — Operating Segments

Source: [IFRS 8](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs-8-operating-segments/)

Permitted use: anchor the management-view basis of reported operating segments
and related disclosure. Paragraph 34 also shows why a major-customer table is
often a thresholded partial view: each customer at or above 10% is disclosed,
while the undisclosed tail is not an implied zero.  Only a view explicitly
established as exhaustive and mutually exclusive should be required to sum to
consolidated revenue; a thresholded customer view remains a concentration
cross-check.

Misuse boundary: reported segments may be too aggregated for causal modeling.
Analytical product, customer or geography branches must reconcile to, not
silently replace, reported totals.  Do not force a partial major-customer
disclosure to 100%, or assume that named customer, product and geography views
are mutually exclusive merely because each is sourced from the same filing.

## Patents, technical readiness and manufacturing

### OECD Patent Statistics Manual

Source: [OECD Patent Statistics Manual](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-patent-statistics-manual_9789264056442-en.html)

Permitted use: support careful use of patent families, applicant/assignee data,
citations and jurisdictional differences in patent indicators.

Misuse boundary: patent counts and citations do not directly measure product
quality, freedom to operate, commercial adoption or economic moat.

### Marx and Fuegi

Source: [Reliance on Science: Worldwide Front-Page Patent Citations to Scientific Articles](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/smj.3145)

Permitted use: support science-to-patent linkage as one way to trace knowledge
flows, with attention to citation construction and coverage.

Misuse boundary: a science citation is not evidence that a patented invention
is manufactured, adopted or profitable.

### NASA and GAO technology-readiness guidance

Sources:

- [NASA Systems Engineering Handbook appendix](https://www.nasa.gov/reference/system-engineering-handbook-appendix/)
- [GAO Technology Readiness Assessment Guide](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-20-48g)

Permitted use: support explicit readiness levels, relevant environments,
evidence and independent assessment of critical technologies.

Misuse boundary: TRL was not designed as a revenue probability or valuation
score. It does not measure manufacturing readiness, qualification, demand or
unit economics.

### DoD Manufacturing Readiness Level Deskbook

Source: [MRL Deskbook 2022](https://www.dodmrl.com/MRL_Deskbook_2022__20221001_Final.pdf)

Permitted use: support separate assessment of manufacturing process maturity,
quality, supply chain, cost and production-scale readiness.

Misuse boundary: MRL is not inferred from TRL and does not prove customer
qualification, commercial demand or accounting recognition.

## Technical-record status, reproducibility and production transfer

### National Academies (2019)

Source: [Reproducibility and Replicability in Science](https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/25303/chapter/3)

Permitted use: keep computational reproducibility distinct from independent
replication and record methods, data, code, uncertainty and failed attempts.

Misuse boundary: inability to reproduce or replicate is not by itself proof
that a claim is false; investigate the cause and cap model permission while the
uncertainty remains unresolved.

### Crossref Crossmark

Source: [Crossmark](https://www.crossref.org/services/crossmark/)

Permitted use: check whether the exact version has a correction, update,
withdrawal or retraction and preserve the dated status source.

Misuse boundary: a current status, DOI or peer review is not a quality score,
independent replication or evidence of commercialization.

### U.S. GAO Technology Readiness Assessment Guide (2020)

Source: [GAO-20-48G](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-20-48g)

Permitted use: discipline technology-readiness evidence, relevant-environment
testing and the gap between laboratory evidence and intended operational use.

Misuse boundary: a readiness level does not estimate factory yield, customer
qualification, revenue probability or accounting timing; those remain separate
company-specific gates.

## Inventory and cycle dynamics

### Lee, Padmanabhan and Whang (1997)

Source: [Information Distortion in a Supply Chain: The Bullwhip Effect](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/abs/10.1287/mnsc.43.4.546)

Permitted use: motivate separating end demand from orders and upstream
production, and testing causes of demand-signal amplification.

Misuse boundary: the bullwhip effect is not a universal explanation for every
inventory move. The case must identify the applicable mechanism and measure its
state variables.

### Semiconductor inventory-cycle study

Source: [Semiconductor inventory and business-cycle study](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.econmod.2012.11.027)

Permitted use: motivate explicit empirical examination of semiconductor
inventory and cycle relationships.

Misuse boundary: aggregate historical relations are not a company forecast or
a timeless lead-lag rule. Product mix, supply discipline, accounting, geography
and regime changes must be tested in the active case.

## Citation discipline

Use these sources to justify method rules only within the permitted scope.
Company forecasts still require point-in-time company, customer, competitor,
industry and technical evidence. Record edition, access date and exact section
when a rule depends on a standard. Paraphrase; do not copy extended passages.
