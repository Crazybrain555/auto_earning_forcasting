# Validated benchmark summary

## Legacy v5 benchmark

The locked legacy set contains 16 historical cutoffs and 96 revenue/profit rows across memory, flash, equipment, foundry, platform chips and a diversified group. v6 leaves legacy forecasts unchanged; regression differences must be exactly zero in the bundled package.

## Expanded v6 value-chain benchmark

Ten additional cutoffs cover:

- Applied Materials;
- KLA;
- Broadcom;
- Vertiv;
- II-VI/Coherent;
- Arista;
- Supermicro;
- Amkor;
- Entegris;
- AMD.

The new cases add equipment/service mix, process-control content, networking concentration, server BOM pass-through, project backlog, advanced packaging, semiconductor materials, optics integration, and acquisition/perimeter bridges.

The benchmark is retrospective point-in-time simulation. It validates method structure and regression control, not live investment performance.


## v6.2 Lam Research / KLA extension

Six historical cutoffs (two calibration, four pseudo-holdout) test wafer-fab-equipment and process-control. On the pseudo-holdout, revenue MAPE improves from 11.3% to 3.7%, profit-margin MAE from 2.47pp to 0.60pp, revenue direction from 66.7% to 91.7%, and normalized interval scores improve 37.7% for revenue and 50.9% for profit.

The Sandisk non-target scope regression has zero deltas across locked revenue, EPS and valuation outputs. These are retrospective simulations, not live out-of-sample results.


## v6.3 Marvell extension

FY2021 is calibration; FY2022/FY2023 are pseudo-holdouts; FY2020 is a distribution-only perimeter break. Holdout revenue MAPE improves 10.9% to 2.6%, operating-margin MAE 12.85pp to 2.33pp, and interval scores improve 45.5%/49.7%. Lam/KLA, Sandisk and legacy regressions remain locked. Retrospective only.


## v7.2 Netflix extension

Netflix FY2014 calibration, FY2016/FY2018 stable holdouts and FY2019 exogenous-shock distributions validate subscriber/ARPU/region/content economics. Stable-holdout revenue MAPE improves from 15.7% to 3.3%, operating-margin MAE from 2.20pp to 0.53pp, and interval scores improve without degrading locked non-target cases. Pandemic-period horizons are evaluated as distribution tails, not point forecasts.

## v7.2 NVIDIA extension

NVIDIA FY2018 calibration, FY2020/FY2021 holdouts and FY2019 perimeter-break distributions validate channel inventory, platform segmentation, supply/inventory provisions, acquisition perimeter and AI regime tails. Holdout revenue MAPE improves from 20.0% to 3.3% and operating-margin MAE from 9.30pp to 1.76pp. Earlier AI and unannounced-acquisition horizons remain distributional.
