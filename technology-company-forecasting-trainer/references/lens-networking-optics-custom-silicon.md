# Lens: networking, optics, and custom silicon

# Networking and server playbook

For networking, model deployments, ports, speeds, ASP, share, supply, customer concentration, RPO/backlog and software/service attach. Separate merchant switching, optical/electrical interconnect silicon, custom ASIC, enterprise, carrier and storage. For Marvell-like businesses also read `optical-custom-silicon.md`.

For servers, revenue is units × BOM/ASP. Split accelerator/memory/networking pass-through from value-added content and model gross-profit dollars, margin, inventory, receivables, deposits and supplier financing. Do not replace a component supplier content model with the finished server/module BOM.

# Optical interconnect and custom silicon playbook

`applies_to`: optical DSPs, coherent/PAM DSPs, TIAs, laser drivers, silicon photonics, DCI, AEC/retimer silicon, merchant switches and custom ASIC/compute programs.

`does_not_apply_to`: finished optical-module assemblers without a semiconductor/content model; commodity memory; wafer-fab equipment; SaaS or cloud services.

## Product and economic perimeter

Do not label the entire business "optical modules." Identify whether revenue is DSP, driver/TIA/PHY, silicon-photonics die/chiplet, DCI chipset/subsystem, AEC/retimer, merchant switch, custom compute/ASIC, storage controller, or a finished module/system actually recognized by the company.

## Program revenue

```text
Program revenue = eligible deployments × content per deployment × supplier share × ASP × stage-conversion probability
```

Do not use a single data-center CAGR. Separate AI optical/custom compute from storage, enterprise, carrier, consumer and automotive inventory states.

## Program stage gate

| Stage | Evidence | Base permission |
|---|---|---|
| 0 architecture | roadmap/collaboration | zero |
| 1 funded NRE | funded engineering | disclosed NRE only |
| 2 tape-out | silicon committed | small tail |
| 3 qualification | samples/validation | limited ramp scenario |
| 4 production award | named/high-confidence award | enter base with ramp |
| 5 material revenue | reported deployment/revenue | full model |

## Supply and recognition

Model foundry, advanced-node capacity, packaging, substrates, optical-component supply and customer readiness. Keep `design win -> NRE -> tape-out -> sample -> qualification -> production shipment -> recognized revenue` separate.

## M&A and perimeter

If announced by `as_of`, model close probability, close date, target standalone revenue, partial consolidation, financing, dilution, inventory fair-value step-up, amortization, integration and restructuring. If not public by `as_of`, do not backfill it; use `distribution-only`, a generic perimeter tail and `human-required` when point output would mislead.

## GAAP bridge

```text
GAAP operating income = adjusted program economics - acquired amortization - inventory step-up - integration - restructuring - SBC - legal/product claims
```

## Regime tail

A convex AI branch needs proposition-appropriate evidence whose independent
measurement roots discriminate it from the serious rival: production
award/material revenue, customer deployment acceleration, capacity commitment,
reported optical/custom growth, or multiple programs/customers may be useful.
No fixed signal count or E-label is itself permission to move the branch into
Base.

# Semiconductor materials and optics playbook

Materials: wafer starts × process steps × content per wafer × share. Optics: design wins × platform units × optical content. Add qualification, yield, utilization, price erosion, material cost and acquisition/perimeter bridges. Do not equate technical bandwidth demand with a named supplier win.
