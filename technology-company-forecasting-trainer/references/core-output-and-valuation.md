# Core output and valuation contracts

## Horizon-specific outputs

### One year

Provide quarterly revenue, profit, cash flow, key KPIs, point estimate, interval, and bridge from company guidance or current run rate.

### Two years

Provide Bear/Base/Bull, probabilities, state transitions, capacity/supply response, and explicit breakpoints. Treat point values as scenario midpoints.

### Three years

Provide probability distribution, product/program options, regime tails, normalized profit, and uncertainty attribution. Do not present a single high-confidence EPS.

### Long term

Use normalized margin, FCF, ROIC, capital intensity, competitive response, and terminal sensitivities. Do not capitalize peak-cycle profit indefinitely.

## Valuation views

Use at least two where material:

1. Near-term/cycle or growth-path value.
2. Long-term normalized value.
3. Scenario or probability-weighted value.
4. Market-implied reverse model.

State what the current price requires in units, usage, price, margin, share, churn, capital intensity, or regime probability.

## Recurring operations versus discrete accounting events

For every material discrete accounting state, show: eligible amount, evidence state, probability by horizon, recognition timing, reported-GAAP impact, normalized/recurring exclusion, cash impact and falsification trigger. Reported GAAP profit distributions must be the mixture of recurring operations and discrete states, not a smoothed effective-tax or margin assumption.

Interval quality must be reported alongside width. A wider interval is an improvement only when it is localized to a supported state and improves proper interval score or coverage without weakening unaffected mechanisms.

## Reported versus normalized profit

When useful-life, capitalization, allocation, restructuring, acquisition accounting, or another accounting estimate materially changes reported profit, show both reported and normalized profit. Do not treat an accounting-estimate benefit as operating leverage without a separate bridge.

For segments without standalone balance sheets or cash flows, label infrastructure and reinvestment schedules as proxies. Do not claim standalone FCF or ROIC without working-capital, lease, tax, cash, and capital-structure allocations.

## Required limitations

State evidence gaps, model allocation choices, unverified customers, unknown product stages, accounting uncertainty, and whether the result is `not-decision-ready`, `screen-grade`, `research-grade`, or stronger.
